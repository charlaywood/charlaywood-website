About This Trip

Jamaica was a whistle-stop four days, which is not nearly enough ‚Äî but four days of Jamaica is still a very good thing. Based in Montego Bay, we packed in as much as the time allowed, and the island delivered at every stop.

Negril was the first excursion: a long stretch of white sand on the western tip of the island, with that particular shade of Caribbean blue that looks slightly unreal until you're standing in it. The water is warm and clear and the beach has the kind of easy, unhurried energy that makes an afternoon disappear without noticing. It's the sort of place that makes you seriously reconsider your life choices ‚Äî the ones that resulted in you living somewhere with winter.

Dunn's River Falls was something else entirely. It's a series of cascading terraced waterfalls that you climb ‚Äî in a human chain, hand in hand with strangers, scrambling over slippery rocks with water rushing down around you. It sounds chaotic, and it is, but it's also joyful and ridiculous and completely worth it. You arrive at the top soaked and grinning and wondering why you don't do things like this more often.

Rick's Cafe, perched on the cliffs at Negril, rounded things off ‚Äî cold drinks, a spectacular sunset, and people leaping off the cliffs into the sea below with varying levels of confidence. Jamaica in four days is a highlights reel. Next time I'd stay longer and go slower.
