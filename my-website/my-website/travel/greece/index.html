About This Trip

Greece is one of those places I keep going back to, and each visit has been different enough to feel like a new country while remaining unmistakably, warmly itself. I've been four times now ‚Äî Athens in 2016, Thessaloniki in 2021, and Crete twice, in 2022 and again in 2025.

Athens was the first, in August 2016, and it set a high bar. The Acropolis is one of those monuments that manages to be more impressive in person than in every photograph you've seen of it ‚Äî the scale, the light, the sheer ancientness of it sitting above a modern city going about its business below. I worked through the historical sites methodically and enthusiastically: the Acropolis Museum, the Ancient Agora, the Temple of Olympian Zeus. Athens rewards the curious.

Thessaloniki in 2021 was a different Greece entirely ‚Äî Greece's second city, less visited than Athens, with a warmth and a food culture that rival anywhere in the country. It has its own remarkable history: Byzantine walls, Ottoman mosques turned into museums, Roman ruins threaded through the city centre. But what I remember most is eating ‚Äî the city's tavernas are exceptional ‚Äî and drinking tsipouro, the Greek pomace spirit that arrives in small glasses alongside small plates of food and disappears faster than you intend.

Crete has had me back twice, and I understand why people return obsessively. The island is enormous by Greek island standards and contains multitudes: dramatic gorges, ancient Minoan palaces, mountain villages that feel untouched, and coastline that ranges from busy resort beaches to hidden coves you reach by scrambling down a path. I've spent time on the beaches, gone jet skiing, visited museums, and eaten and drunk very well. The tsipouro flows in Crete too ‚Äî usually accompanied by a small plate of something and a warmth of hospitality that makes it very hard to leave.

Greece has a way of feeling both ancient and alive simultaneously ‚Äî the history is everywhere but so is the pleasure in the present moment. It keeps pulling me back, and I don't expect that to change.
